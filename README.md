# Snake-Game
One thing I really wanted to do was keep the snake game pretty similar to what i remembered from when i played it as a kid and keep the 
arcade and techno feel that it originally had. After playing with the original product developed by the seed prompts, it was clear right away that the transitions between games was happening too suddenly. This made me want to add the start button to break it up, and the countdown timer before the game so the player has more time to mentally prepare. I felt that the visual and audio of the game was also just as important to make the game have the arcade feel as well. I added a countdown tune which i wanted to be somewhat similar to the beginning of a Mario Kart race which i was satisfied with when it turned out. Adding a small sound effect when the snake ran into a piece of food was also fun and i believe adds to the games immersion while you're playing it. I also remember the game having the food represented as something like an apple or natural when i played it and wanted to keep that consistent. Naturally, i wanted to make the snake look more like a snake as well so adding eyes and a forked tongue made it look cooler. I also made sure to visually keep the food and the snake brighter colors that could contrast to the background to make it easier for the player to keep track of both objects when they are playing.
